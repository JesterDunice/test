Specifying content in a stylesheet can cause complications. For example, you might have different language versions of your document that share a stylesheet. If part of the stylesheet has to be translated, it means that you must put those parts of the stylesheet in separate files and arrange for them to be linked with the appropriate language versions of your document. These complications do not arise if the content you specify consists of symbols or images that apply in all languages and cultures. Content specified in a stylesheet does not become part of the DOM.
